Ready To Overcrook

Sometimes simple is best. For Sean Malto the classic overcrook is one of the best tricks there is. The beauty of overcrook is you can go up stuff, down stuff and around stuff too. Before you go for it, be sure you're going fast and check out how slick the rail is. Once you're ready, use Sean's three tips to nail the trick.

Heel side first

Approach any obstacle on your heel side to be in prime position. Once you're there, pop an ollie and turn 45 o so the main area of your board makes good contact.

Find your balance

Now you're overcrooking, so ride it out. Try to keep your center of gravity straight over the board to balance as long as you can. This is when it's important to know how slick the rail is, becasue you'll need to adjust your balance to cope.

Pop out

Every rail is different, so it's on you to know when to pop out and ride away. If the rail enableFriendscleanly, you can just flat off it. But if it doesn't, you'll need to lightly dig with your heels, shimmy your board off and use your momentum to keep on rolling
